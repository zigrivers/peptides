Recovery · Tissue Repair · Gut

BPC-157

$115
200 mg total · 10 × 20 mg

Body Protection Compound — 15. A 15-amino-acid synthetic peptide derived from a protective protein in human gastric juice. The most-used peptide in the 50+ active population because the bottleneck at this age is recovery, not work capacity.

It accelerates tendon-to-bone and ligament healing, restores gut lining (especially helpful with NSAID damage or GERD), modulates inflammation through nitric oxide pathways, and upregulates growth-hormone receptors locally at injured tissue — meaning systemic GH/IGF-1 has a bigger effect at the injury site.

Aggressive protocol

Dose 500 mcg per injection
Frequency 2× daily (AM + PM) = 1 mg/day
Reconstitute 20 mg vial + 2 mL bac water → 10 mg/mL
Syringe 5 units per injection (U-100 insulin)
Injection site Subq abdomen, or local near injured tissue
Supply duration 200 days continuous (~6.5 months)
Expected benefits timeline
  • Week 1–2Subtle GI improvements if present (reflux, bloating); reduced inflammation markers
  • Week 3–4Faster post-workout recovery, noticeable reduction in joint pain
  • Week 6–8Tendon and ligament healing apparent (if treating an injury)
  • Month 3+Sustained tissue remodeling, improved baseline joint comfort
Safety profile Excellent. No serious adverse effects documented in animal models or anecdotal human use. Mild injection site reactions are the only common complaint. Suggested cycling: 6 weeks on, 2–4 weeks off to prevent receptor desensitization, though continuous use is also reported as well-tolerated.
Local vs systemic For a specific joint or tendon, inject 5 units subq as close to the affected tissue as practical. For systemic effects (gut, general inflammation), abdomen subq is fine.

GLP-1 / Amylin · Weight Loss

Cagrilintide / Semaglutide mix

$130
100 mg total · 10 × 10 mg
Conflict Do not run this concurrently with tirzepatide (prescribed or gray-market). Both heavily activate the GLP-1 receptor. Stacking creates compounded gastric, pancreatic, and gallbladder risk without proportional benefit. Choose one weight-loss path.

The combination of cagrilintide (long-acting amylin analog) and semaglutide (GLP-1 agonist) — Novo Nordisk's experimental obesity drug "CagriSema." In Phase 3 REDEFINE trials, the combination produced approximately 22% body weight loss at 68 weeks at maximum dose. The mechanism is dual: GLP-1 receptor activation (appetite suppression, gastric emptying delay, insulin response) plus amylin receptor activation (separate satiety pathway, glucagon suppression).

Aesthetic · Collagen · Hair

GLOW50

$150
500 mg total · 10 × 50 mg
Composition unknown — verify with vendor "GLOW50" is a vendor-specific blend, not a standardized peptide. Most GLOW formulations are predominantly GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide-1). Some include BPC-157 and/or TB-500. Request the exact composition from the vendor before dosing. Protocol below assumes pure GHK-Cu at 50 mg per vial.

GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine + copper) is a naturally occurring copper-binding peptide that declines sharply with age — plasma levels at 60 are roughly 30% of what they were at 20. It activates fibroblasts (collagen, elastin, glycosaminoglycan synthesis), regulates over 4,000 human genes (gene-expression "reset" effects), stimulates hair follicle proliferation, and has wound-healing and antioxidant activity.

GH Axis · Visceral Fat · Body Comp

Tesamorelin

$170
100 mg total · 10 × 10 mg

A growth-hormone-releasing-hormone (GHRH) analog, FDA-approved as Egrifta for HIV-associated lipodystrophy. Of all GH peptides, Tesamorelin has the strongest clinical evidence base for visceral adipose tissue (VAT) reduction — the dangerous belly fat that surrounds organs and drives metabolic disease.

It binds GHRH receptors in the anterior pituitary, stimulating endogenous, pulsatile GH release — meaning IGF-1 rises within a physiological pattern rather than the supra-physiological constant elevation that exogenous HGH produces. This makes it safer at age 56 than direct HGH.

Aggressive protocol

Dose 2 mg per injection (FDA-approved Egrifta dose)
Frequency Daily, evening (10pm or pre-bed)
Reconstitute 10 mg vial + 2 mL bac water → 5 mg/mL
Syringe 40 units (= 2 mg) once daily
Injection site Subq abdomen, rotate quadrants daily
Supply duration 50 days continuous · ~10 weeks with 5-on/2-off cycling
Expected benefits timeline
  • Week 1–2Sleep quality and depth improve (GH pulse normalization)
  • Week 3–4Subjective body comp shifts begin (slight reduction in midsection)
  • Week 6–8Measurable VAT reduction on imaging; waist circumference shrinks
  • Week 12–24Sustained VAT loss; improved triglycerides; better skin quality
Critical: monitor IGF-1 Baseline IGF-1 before starting. Recheck at week 8. Target: upper-normal for age (typically 150–250 ng/mL at 56). Above this, cancer-promotion concerns increase, especially prostate. Get a baseline PSA too.

Pineal · Sleep · Longevity

Epitalon

$60
100 mg total · 10 × 10 mg

A synthetic tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) developed by Russian gerontologist Vladimir Khavinson, modeled on a natural pineal extract called epithalamin. It is thought to restore age-related pineal function — most importantly melatonin synthesis, which crashes by ~50% between ages 30 and 60. Animal and Russian clinical studies also report telomerase activation, though the Western evidence base is limited.

For a 56-year-old, the sleep effect alone justifies a cycle. Melatonin replacement (oral pills) is downstream; Epitalon may restore upstream pineal production capacity.

Mitochondrial · Metabolic · Longevity

MOTS-c

$250
400 mg total · 10 × 40 mg

A 16-amino-acid peptide encoded within mitochondrial DNA — making it one of the few peptides that originates inside the cell's energy organelle and signals out to the nucleus to regulate gene expression. It activates AMPK (the metabolic master switch), improves insulin sensitivity, drives mitochondrial biogenesis, and enhances fat oxidation during exercise.

Of all the peptides on this stack, MOTS-c has arguably the strongest mechanistic rationale for healthy aging — mitochondrial decline is a primary driver of age-related metabolic disease, and MOTS-c levels themselves drop significantly with age.

§ 11

Injection logistics

Supplies needed

  • Syringes: BD U-100 insulin syringes, 31G × 5/16" or 8mm, 0.3 mL or 0.5 mL barrel. Buy in bulk (100-count box ~$25 on Amazon or pharmacy).
  • Bacteriostatic water: 0.9% sodium chloride with 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative. 30 mL vials. Order from compounding pharmacies (Empower, Cedar, Hallandale) or research-grade suppliers.
  • Alcohol prep pads: Standard medical, sterile, individually wrapped. Box of 200, ~$5.
  • Sharps container: 1-quart minimum. Local pharmacies sell or accept.
  • Refrigerator space: Dedicated shelf, 36–46°F (2–8°C). Do not store in door (temperature fluctuates).

Standard subq injection technique

  1. Wash hands. Alcohol-swab vial top and injection site, let air-dry 15 seconds.
  2. Draw correct unit volume into syringe. Tap to remove air bubbles, push to zero plunger.
  3. Pinch 1–2 inches of subcutaneous tissue at injection site (abdomen, 2+ inches from navel).
  4. Insert needle at 45° (thin) or 90° (average) angle, fully.
  5. Release pinch, push plunger slowly over 3–5 seconds.
  6. Withdraw needle, brief pressure with cotton if bleeding.
  7. Dispose of syringe in sharps container — never recap.

Site rotation

Abdomen has four usable quadrants (upper-left, upper-right, lower-left, lower-right of navel). Each site needs 7–10 days recovery before reuse to prevent lipodystrophy and scarring. For multiple daily injections, use different quadrants AM vs PM. Thighs and upper buttocks are secondary sites.

§ 12

Vial shelf life & storage

The most important fact: lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptides are far more stable than people fear during shipping, and far more fragile once reconstituted than people assume. Degradation requires water — the freeze-drying process removes it, which is why a powder can survive a warm transit but a reconstituted vial cannot survive a hot car. Understanding the lyophilized → bac water → reconstituted timeline is the single highest-yield piece of operational knowledge for this stack.

Step 1 — Upon receipt: inspect before storing

Every shipment gets a 60-second inspection before anything goes in the fridge. This is your only chance to identify a problem batch and request replacement.

CheckPassFail / contact vendor
Package exterior Intact, no water damage, no crushing Visibly damaged, soaked, or punctured
Cold pack status (if included) Cool to slightly warm — expected after transit Hot, or pack ruptured/leaking
Vial seal Crimped aluminum cap intact, no tampering signs Cap loose, lifted, or appears refitted
Powder appearance White to off-white fluffy powder or compact "puck" at vial bottom Visibly wet, melted, yellow/brown discoloration, clumped wet, partially dissolved
GLOW50 (if GHK-Cu) White-to-very-faint-blue powder Deep blue or green = oxidized copper, likely degraded
Labeling / lot number Matches Janoshik COA / lab report supplied Missing label, lot mismatch, no COA
Liquid present None — vials should be dry powder only Any visible liquid = stopper failure, contaminated
Transit warming is OK A package that arrives at room temp — or even briefly warm — does not mean the peptides are degraded. Lyophilized peptides tolerate brief exposure to 40–50°C (104–122°F) with minimal degradation because hydrolysis requires water that isn't present. The cold pack is a safety margin, not a hard requirement. What matters far more: appearance, seal, and how quickly you get them into refrigeration after arrival.

Step 5 — The equilibration rule (most-overlooked step)

Before opening a cold vial Let it sit at room temperature for 15–30 minutes until it's no longer cold to the touch. If you open a refrigerated or frozen vial immediately, warm humid air condenses inside the vial onto the powder — introducing the very moisture the lyophilization was designed to eliminate. This is the single most common (and most consequential) handling mistake.

This rule applies to both initial reconstitution and every subsequent draw from a reconstituted vial. The 15-minute equilibration is cheap insurance against accumulated moisture damage across multiple uses.

Bacteriostatic water

Once the rubber stopper is first punctured, the USP-recommended use period is 28 days. The 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative inhibits bacterial growth during that window. After 28 days, discard regardless of remaining volume. Unopened: stable at room temp until the manufacturer's expiration date (typically 2–5 years).

Reconstitution procedure — step by step

This is the detailed procedure for every peptide vial. Once familiar, it takes about 5 minutes. Sterile technique matters — you're creating an injectable solution, and contamination introduced at this stage can't be undone.

Supplies to set out before starting
  • Bacteriostatic water, 30 mL vial (0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative)
  • U-100 insulin syringes, 31G × 5/16" (8mm), 1 mL or 0.5 mL barrel — minimum two: one for drawing bac water, one for dosing
  • Alcohol prep pads, sterile, individually wrapped
  • Sharps container within arm's reach
  • Sharpie + masking tape or small labels for marking reconstitution and discard dates
  • Clean, flat work surface — kitchen counter wiped with alcohol, paper towel underneath
Step-by-step
  1. Equilibrate. Take both vials (peptide + bac water) out of the fridge and let them sit at room temp for 15–30 minutes until no longer cold to the touch. Skip this and you'll condense moisture into the vial when you puncture it.
  2. Prep workspace. Wash hands thoroughly. Wipe the work surface with alcohol. Lay out all supplies before opening anything.
  3. Swab vial stoppers. Use a fresh alcohol pad on each vial's rubber stopper. Let air-dry 15 seconds — don't blow on it (introduces breath moisture and bacteria).
  4. Pressure-equalize the bac water vial. Pull air into the empty syringe equal to the volume of bac water you'll withdraw (e.g., 2 mL of air for 2 mL of water). Insert into the bac water vial, push the air in, then invert the vial and slowly withdraw your bac water volume. This prevents vacuum, which causes drips and inaccurate measurements.
  5. Insert into the peptide vial at 45°. Position the needle bevel against the inner glass wall above the powder, not directly aimed at it.
  6. Trickle, don't squirt. Push the plunger slowly so bac water runs down the side of the vial onto the powder. Direct impact on lyophilized powder can denature peptides.
  7. Withdraw the needle, set the vial down. Do not shake. Shaking creates foam and shears peptide structure.
  8. Swirl, don't agitate. Gently roll or swirl the vial in a circular motion every 30 seconds. Most peptides fully dissolve in 1–5 minutes; some larger or stubborn ones take up to 15.
  9. If slow to dissolve: warm gently in your palms for 60 seconds at a time. Never microwave, run under warm water, or heat above body temperature.
  10. Inspect the solution. Should be clear or only faintly colored (GHK-Cu blue; everything else colorless). Cloudiness, particles, or unusual color = discard and use a fresh vial.
  11. Label the vial. Write reconstitution date and 28-day discard date on tape or directly on the glass with Sharpie. Example: "Recon 5/20 · Discard 6/17."
  12. Store upright in the back of the fridge, away from light. For GHK-Cu specifically, wrap the vial in aluminum foil or keep in a small opaque container — copper is light-sensitive.
  13. Dispose of the syringe in your sharps container. Never recap a used needle (needlestick risk).
Pro tips that prevent the most common mistakes
  • The 15-minute equilibration applies to every dose pull, not just initial reconstitution. Cold vial + warm room air = condensation inside the vial. Every time you open a stored vial, let it warm first.
  • One syringe = one purpose. Use a separate syringe for drawing bac water vs. drawing your dose. Don't reuse syringes between different peptide vials — cross-contamination is a real risk.
  • Pressure-equalize every draw. Even pulling small dose volumes, briefly inject a matching volume of air before withdrawing. Reduces drips and improves measurement accuracy at small volumes.
  • If powder won't fully dissolve after 15 minutes, it likely absorbed moisture during storage. The vial is compromised — use another.
  • Bubbles are normal during dissolution. Wait — they'll settle on their own. Don't tap or flick aggressively to dislodge them; that's shearing the peptide.
  • Reconstitute only what you'll use within ~28 days. Leave the rest as lyophilized powder in the fridge or freezer where they remain stable for months to years.

Reconstitution math for blend vials

Single-peptide vials are simple: choose a bac water volume that gives clean unit math. Blend vials (GLOW, CagriSema, any combo) require calculating per-component dosing per unit. The principle: dose for the limiting (lowest-mass) peptide in the blend, then accept the proportional doses of the other components.

Worked example — GHK-Cu / BPC-157 / TB-500 blend (60mg + 10mg + 10mg = 80mg total)
  • Reconstitute 80 mg with 2 mL bac water → 40 mg/mL total mass
  • Per unit (0.01 mL): 300 mcg GHK-Cu + 50 mcg BPC-157 + 50 mcg TB-500
  • 10 units = 3 mg GHK-Cu + 500 mcg BPC-157 + 500 mcg TB-500 (one full BPC-157 dose, with proportional doses of the other two — a complete healing cocktail in one injection)
Worked example — CagriSema mix (assumes 1:1 cagri:sema, 10mg total)
  • Reconstitute 10 mg with 1 mL bac water → 10 mg/mL total mass
  • Per unit (0.01 mL): 50 mcg cagrilintide + 50 mcg semaglutide
  • 48 units = 2.4 mg cagri + 2.4 mg sema (full Phase 3 target weekly dose)
Always confirm the ratio For any blend, confirm the actual component ratio from the vendor's Janoshik COA before dosing. Assumed ratios can be wrong, and "1:1 by weight" is not the same thing as "1:1 by moles." The math above only holds if the label matches the lab report.

Inspect before every dose. Any of the following = discard the vial, regardless of remaining content or how recent.

Supporting supplements

Specifically chosen to address the failure modes of this stack — not generic "wellness" stack-padding.

Mandatory (addresses specific risks)

  • Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine), 25–50 mg daily — Replaces what the Cedar compounded version provides. Nausea control on GLP-1s.
  • Methylcobalamin (B12), 1 mg sublingual daily — GLP-1s reduce B12 absorption; B12 deficiency is sneaky and can cause neuropathy.
  • Protein supplement (whey or casein) — Reach 1 g/lb body weight daily target. Lean mass preservation is the central risk of this stack.
  • Creatine monohydrate, 5 g daily — Cheap, well-evidenced lean mass preservation aid, especially valuable during GLP-1-driven weight loss.
  • Magnesium glycinate, 300–400 mg PM — Common deficiency; supports sleep, glucose handling, neuromuscular function.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2, 5,000 IU + 100 mcg daily — Bone density during weight loss, immune function.

Optional but logical additions

  • Berberine, 500 mg 2× daily — AMPK activator like MOTS-c via different mechanism; mild additional glucose benefit.
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA), 2–3 g daily — Anti-inflammatory; cardiovascular support during weight loss.
  • Bovine colostrum, 1 tbsp daily — Gut barrier support; complements BPC-157 effects.
  • Taurine, 1–3 g daily — Supports bile flow (important during rapid weight loss with gallstone risk); cardiovascular and longevity benefits.
Avoid Avoid melatonin pills during Epitalon cycles — Epitalon's mechanism may be partially via upstream restoration of endogenous melatonin synthesis. Exogenous melatonin may suppress this signal. Resume melatonin between cycles if used.
