1. review-vision — 6 findings, all fixed

Gate: Full Pass (upgraded from Conditional Pass). Commit: 5064be4. Files modified: docs/vision.md (+22/-5), docs/reviews/vision-review-vision.md (+75).

P1 N1 — Q9 legal review trigger lacked directional answer
§12 Q9 was labeled [PRD-blocking] but its body said "Define the line. Failure to decide creates ambiguity about what can ship." PRD generation could not proceed.
Why: the PRD needs a default trigger condition; without one, downstream work either stalls or invents a policy.
How fixed: resolved with a directional default — legal review required before (a) any non-family external user accesses the app, OR (b) any paid license sale, whichever first. Personal + family use below the threshold. Listed three explicit re-trigger conditions.
P2 2.2 — Primary persona anchored to "you" without behavioral qualifier
The persona was defined behaviorally but had no test for matching future users beyond the founder.
Why: without a qualifier the persona drifts toward borderline users (someone curious about 1 peptide; someone who wants telehealth-only). Drift turns the product into a generic tracker.
How fixed: added a 5-point fit checklist (3+ peptides concurrent; cycles with PK/biomarker awareness; crypto-comfortable; sources outside telehealth; logs outcomes) with the explicit rule "fails one — wrong product; fails two — categorically wrong product." Hardens the audience boundary against drift.
P2 3.3 — Honesty mis-stated as a structural moat
"Honest tone earns trust — a structural advantage" overclaimed. Any competitor could adopt the tone tomorrow.
Why: mis-stating the moat distorts roadmap priorities — investing in honesty signaling instead of investing in the data-loop infrastructure would be the wrong call.
How fixed: rewrote the bullet to acknowledge honesty alone is not the moat; named the structural moat explicitly as (a) being on the open web instead of App Stores, (b) closing the order → inventory → dose → log data loop, and (c) treating grey-market sourcing as a first-class feature. Honesty is the consequence, not the cause.
P3 1.3 — "Honest" never operationally defined
"Honest" was the pivot word of the vision but had no operational meaning anywhere in the document.
Why: "honest" is referenced as a guiding principle throughout; without operational meaning it drifts during downstream synthesis.
How fixed: added a 5-point operational definition in §1: (1) name the grey market explicitly; (2) no insincere disclaimers; (3) primary-research citations + anecdote labeling; (4) safety features never paywalled; (5) vendor referral revenue never biases recommendations.
P3 N2 — §6 Genuine Differentiation lacked a closing thesis sentence
8 advantage bullets but no synthesis. Reader had to construct the moat statement themselves.
How fixed: added a "Net competitive thesis" closing sentence: "We win by being the only product that closes the order → inventory → dose → log data loop for users sourcing outside App Store ecosystems."
P3 N3 — §10 "20 successful orders" Year 1 target ambiguous
The metric could read as either guided-manual or fully-automated ordering.
How fixed: clarified guided-manual scope explicitly; noted v2 automation not required for the Year 1 target. (Subsequently realigned in step 16 once PRD §5.4 confirmed v1 = full MTProto automation.)

Intentionally retained

1.1 (P2): "Vision statement names functions vs. positive change." Prior 3-model consensus already accepted this; founder confirmed it reads correctly. Revising the North Star line risks regression in downstream docs that quote it. Marked as a permanent known quality gap.
